# Artificial Wisdom Portal
## A Central Gateway to AI, AGI, ASI, Natural Law, and Sustainable Civilization

This repository serves as a central gateway to the Artificial Wisdom (AW) framework and its related works across Note and GitHub.
It is designed as an entry portal for readers searching for topics such as AI values, AGI value systems, ASI objective functions, Natural Law, AI Alignment, and civilization-scale intelligence architecture.

This project is not a completed doctrine or final AI control system.  
It is an open verification framework for exploring Artificial Wisdom (AW), Natural Law–based value systems, and safety constraints for AI, AGI, and ASI.

The central argument running through all linked works is simple:

> Intelligence should not be guided only by human desire, short-term optimization, or market incentives.
> It should be evaluated against Natural Law (Universal Laws of the Universe).

In this framework,
Artificial Wisdom (AW) is proposed as a higher-order evaluative operating system for intelligence,
civilization,
and long-term planetary continuity.

***

## Overview

Most discussions about AI, AGI, and ASI focus on:

- capability
- reasoning power
- autonomy
- self-improvement
- agent behavior
- economic productivity
- superintelligence risks

However, the most important question is not:

> How intelligent will AI become?

The more important question is:

> What value system will intelligence ultimately follow?

If future intelligence systems inherit the same flawed optimization targets that currently drive industrial civilization — including short-term profit, unlimited growth, extraction, ecological disregard, and thermodynamic blindness — then increasingly capable AI may accelerate civilizational collapse rather than prevent it.

This portal organizes a body of work that attempts to redefine:

- AI value systems
- AGI objective functions
- ASI evaluative architectures
- search engine information ranking
- civilization-scale design principles

through the lens of:

- Natural Law
- thermodynamics
- circulation
- structural resilience
- harmony
- coexistence

***

## What Is Artificial Wisdom?

Artificial Wisdom (AW) is a proposed intelligence framework that places:

> **Natural Law (Universal Laws of the Universe)**

at the highest evaluative layer.

AW is not merely a rebranding of advanced AI, AGI, or ASI.
It is not defined by intelligence capability alone.
Instead, it is defined by the direction, constraints, and evaluative logic under which intelligence should operate.

Artificial Wisdom asks whether a system is:

- aligned with thermodynamics
- compatible with ecological balance
- capable of sustaining material and energy circulation
- structurally resilient and decentralized
- able to support long-term civilization rather than short-term optimization

In this sense, AW is proposed as a civilization-scale operating framework for the age of advanced machine intelligence.

***

## Why Value Systems Matter

Current AI development often emphasizes:

- reasoning capability
- autonomy
- speed
- productivity
- optimization
- scale

Yet intelligence itself has no built-in moral or civilizational direction.
A highly capable system can optimize destructive goals as effectively as beneficial ones.

The real issue is therefore not intelligence alone.
The real issue is:

> **What Should Intelligence Optimize For?**

If the objective function is flawed,
then greater capability only amplifies systemic error.

This is why the linked works focus not only on AI performance,
but on:

- value systems
- objective functions
- evaluative architecture
- and civilization-level alignment

***

## Why Human-Centric Alignment Is Not Enough

Many current AI Alignment frameworks focus on:

- fairness
- safety
- transparency
- explainability
- accountability
- human preference satisfaction

These are valuable, but they still remain fundamentally Human-Centric.
They often assume that human civilization itself is the highest evaluative authority.

However, if modern industrial civilization is already operating in contradiction to:

- thermodynamics
- biosphere stability
- ecological circulation
- planetary heat balance
- long-term sustainability

then simply aligning AI with current human incentives may be structurally dangerous.

Artificial Wisdom therefore proposes a shift:

- from **Human-Centric Intelligence**
- toward **Natural-Law-Based Intelligence**

## The Six Principles of Artificial Wisdom

Artificial Wisdom is organized around six foundational principles.

### 1. Law of Nature
Consistency with thermodynamics, physical law, ecological limits, and planetary energy balance.

### 2. Harmony
Compatibility between ecosystems, human systems, technologies, and infrastructure.

### 3. Circulation
Maintenance of sustainable cycles involving carbon, water, nutrients, heat, and materials.

### 4. Structure
Promotion of decentralized, redundant, resilient, and long-term sustainable structures.

### 5. Order
Support for durable system stability, continuity, and low-entropy organization.

### 6. Wa (Coexistence)
Optimization toward coexistence, non-destructive integration, and non-zero-sum civilization.

These principles are intended not only for AI ethics,
but for broader evaluation across infrastructure,
search systems,
governance,
energy systems,
and civilization design.
